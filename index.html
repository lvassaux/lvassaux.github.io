Research

Themes and questions

I study the probabilistic structure of high-dimensional inference problems, especially where sparsity and symmetry create sharp transitions between the possible and the impossible.

01

Graph alignment

Information-theoretic thresholds, partial recovery, and the geometry of the posterior in correlated random graphs.

02

Random graphs

Sparse and inhomogeneous models, balanced loads, cores, and structural parameters governing recoverability.

03

High-dimensional statistics

Bayesian estimation, computational barriers, phase transitions, and connections with statistical physics.

04

Probability

Limit theorems and probabilistic methods motivated by random matrices, number theory, and combinatorics.
